Course Description

Study of federal income taxation as applied to regular corporations, S corporations, partnerships, trusts and estates, plus a brief introduction to tax research and federal wealth taxes.

Required Materials

Southwestern Federal Taxation, Comprehensive Volume by Hoffman, Maloney, Raabe, and Young. Depending on when you purchased the textbook, you may have the 2019, 2020, or 2021 edition. I will try to make assessments for all three editions available, so that you can complete assessments based on your textbook edition. However, I will use the 2021 edition in class meetings.

Course Learning Objectives

This course will provide you with an overview of the income taxation of regular corporations, partnerships, S corporations, trusts, and estates, as well as an introduction to wealth transfer tax. After completing this course, you will:

  • Be familiar with the tax life cycle of a corporation, partnership, and S corporation.
  • Be familiar with tax issues related to trusts and estates.
  • Have the ability to read and interpret the Internal Revenue Code and other sources of tax law.

Exams:

The course includes three equally weighted examinations. Exam dates appear in the Detailed Tentative Schedule portion of this syllabus. Exams will be available all day on the day reported in the schedule. Exams will draw from CPA Review multiple-choice questions, other multiple-choice questions, and exercises similar to those used in the homework assignments. The first exam will be a practice exam based on review material. The practice exam will not count toward your final grade, but I will award some extra credit toward your overall exam grade for completing it.

All exams will be proctored using Proctorio. Proctorio allows students with a webcam, a microphone, and a broadband internet connection to complete their exams while on camera; the service does not use live proctors but instead uses an automated algorithm to report on any exam session irregularities. The service is not free to students. Students may be charged up to $10 per exam in each course (Discounts may occasionally be in effect and are not under the control of Truman). Since this course has more than 2 proctored exams, selecting the "course fee" model instead of the "individual test fee" model when paying for the first exam will result in a price equal to two individual exams. Subsequent exams for that course bear no additional cost.

Homework:

Homework assignments will be administered through Blackboard. The purpose of each homework assignment is to reinforce the material from the textbook in order to prepare you for the course exams and for the CPA exam. As a result, each homework assignment will include some or all of the following: CPA Review multiple-choice questions, additional multiple-choice questions, and exercises. After most class periods, you will have a homework assignment due before the next class day.

Absence Policy

No student having a sanctioned absence shall be penalized for such absence on account solely of being absent. Nothing in this policy is intended to excuse a student from the responsibility to make up missed work within a reasonable length of time. Students with planned absences should notify me prior to an absence from class. Students with unanticipated absences should notify me as early as possible. Documentation of any absence may be required at my discretion.

If you must miss an exam due to a planned sanctioned absence, you will need to pre-arrange a time for you to take the exam. Otherwise, exams must be taken when scheduled except for unplanned sanctioned absences (which should be scheduled for make-up as soon as possible). Missed quizzes cannot be made up for any reason except in the case of a prolonged, medical absence from the university or other similar situation as determined by me. Late assignments and homework are generally not accepted given the length of time given to complete.

Definition of Sanctioned Absences: Absences of students from scheduled classes will be considered sanctioned for the following listed reasons:

  • Participation as a representative of the University in a scheduled intercollegiate athletic event.
  • Participation as a representative of the University in a scheduled professional/academic conference, academic competition, or a field trip scheduled as part of a course.
  • Participation as an officer of a University co-curricular organization in a scheduled conference for which participation is mandatory for the student (e.g., a required annual meeting for all presidents of a national student organization).
  • Professional or graduate school interviews as well as professional job interviews that cannot be rescheduled.
  • Health related absences.
  • Absences covered by Truman’s non-discrimination policy (e.g. special religious observances, military commitments).
  • Other situations not specifically noted in this list, but approved by me or the Provost.

Academic Honesty

Academic integrity is a standard set for this course. Either giving help to or receiving help from anyone else (whether that person is a member of our class or not) is a violation of this policy. Students are expected to complete all of their coursework and assignments individually unless otherwise assigned. Students are expected to work alone on the exams without the use of any outside resources. Students are encouraged to consult each other on homework assignments. Students are expected to be honest in their interactions with the professor. Violations will result in a zero on the offending assignment/exam and may lead to other penalties, including failure of the course. A student not upholding these expectations is subject to being reported to the Dean of Student Affairs Office and the Vice President for Academic Affairs. If you have any questions, please ask.
