Course Overview and Expected Learning Outcomes

Accounting 3304 is the first intermediate financial accounting course. The series of intermediate accounting courses is designed primarily for preparers of financial statements, but both classes are also valuable for users of financial statements, who must understand the complexities of both US GAAP and IFRS in order to use financial accounting information to conduct meaningful economic analyses. If you plan for a career in accounting, auditing, or finance; corporate taxation or management consulting; or even data analytics, this is one of the most important courses you will take in your undergraduate program.

This course provides a rigorous treatment of financial accounting concepts and techniques, including the balance sheet equation, the four primary financial statements, pre- and post-closing trial balances, journal entries, and the general ledger. These resources provide a means for summarizing the economic impact of a vast array of business events and transactions.

Intermediate I introduces financial statement concepts and the basic financial statements, and then begins an in-depth examination of various balance sheet accounts. This examination then continues in Intermediate II. The most you invest in this course, the more prepared you will be for Intermediate II and your future career. You will get out of this course what you put into it. It is vital that you understand the material, not just memorize.

As unpopular as it might be, understanding the material begins with reading the textbook and completing exercises. The lectures and deliverables will not substitute for a proper, self-motivated study of the textbook, but I commit to doing my best to using class periods and deliverables to maximize the value of a personal study of the textbook. If you will work with me, I will help you to have a successful experience.

Course Deliverables

Quizzes

Quizzes will be administered through Blackboard. After covering a chapter, you will have a quiz that is due by 11:00 am on the day reported in the schedule. Each quiz will contain true-false and/or multiple choice questions.

Midterm Exams

The course includes three equally weighted midterm examinations. Exam dates appear in the Tentative Schedule portion of this syllabus. At the end of the semester, you may replace your lowest test score of the three midterms with the same grade earned on the Final Exam if the Final Exam grade is higher than the lowest test score.

Final Exam

A final exam will be given during the scheduled final exam period. The date and time can be found in the Tentative Schedule portion of this syllabus. The material covered will be comprehensive.

Bonus: Memos

I want us to work well together, so I will ask for memos from you during the semester for two reasons. First, I want to get to know you, so I will ask for you to introduce yourself to me in a memo. Second, I value continuous improvement. The purpose of this course is to help you to improve as professionals, but I also want to improve as a teacher. Throughout the semester, I will ask for feedback from you on how this course is going and how I am performing. I will give extra credit for each evaluation, including for the student evaluations that you provide at the end of the semester. I am not embarrassed to admit that I want you to give me good evaluations at the end of the semester, and I hope that your feedback during the semester will help me to earn good evaluations from you at the end of the semester.

I will not dictate a format for these memos, but I will invite you to use this experience to practice your professional writing skills, so please use correct grammar and spelling and a professional writing style.

Ungraded: Exercises

For most chapters, I will identify end-of-chapter homework exercises that serve to reinforce the learning experience. You may complete these exercises in WileyPlus, or you may complete them using the textbook. I will not grade homework, but I will review the exercises in class to the extent possible. I can also review exercises with you during my office hours.
