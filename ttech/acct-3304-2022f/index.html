Course Overview and Expected Learning Outcomes

Accounting 3304 is the first intermediate financial accounting course. The series of intermediate accounting courses is designed primarily for preparers of financial statements, but both classes are also valuable for users of financial statements, who must understand the complexities of both US GAAP and IFRS in order to use financial accounting information to conduct meaningful economic analyses. If you plan for a career in accounting, auditing, or finance; corporate taxation or management consulting; or even data analytics, this is one of the most important courses you will take in your undergraduate program.

This course provides a rigorous treatment of financial accounting concepts and techniques, including the balance sheet equation, the four primary financial statements, pre- and post-closing trial balances, journal entries, and the general ledger. These resources provide a means for summarizing the economic impact of a vast array of business events and transactions.

Intermediate I introduces financial statement concepts and the basic financial statements, and then begins an in-depth examination of various balance sheet accounts. This examination then continues in Intermediate II. The most you invest in this course, the more prepared you will be for Intermediate II and your future career. You will get out of this course what you put into it. It is vital that you understand the material, not just memorize.

As unpopular as it might be, understanding the material begins with reading the textbook. The lectures and deliverables will not substitute for a proper, self-motivated study of the textbook, but I commit to doing my best to using class periods and deliverables to maximize the value of a personal study of the textbook. If you will work with me, I will help you to have a successful experience.

Course Requirements

Homework & Quizzes

Quizzes and homework assignments will generally be administered through the online software MyLab Accounting. Following most class periods, you will have a homework assignment that is due by 12:00 pm on the day reported in the schedule. After covering a chapter, you will have a quiz that is due by 12:00 pm on the day reported in the schedule. You should expect to complete at least one deliverable after each class period prior to the next class period. Instructions for how to register for the class through MyLab Accounting is found in this handout: Student Registration Handout. The website for MyLab Accounting is www.pearson.com/mylab.

Each quiz will contain true-false and/or multiple choice questions. Each homework assignment will include some or all of the following: multiple-choice questions, definitions, matching, calculations, open-ended questions, and Excel assignments. If you do not have access to Microsoft Excel for these assignments, then you can use the free version of Excel Online that comes with every Live.com or Outlook.com account. Unfortunately, you cannot use the Excel editor in Google Drive for these assignments because the grading tool cannot recognize the file. Alternatively, you can use the virtual Windows client through view.truman.edu. After selecting VMware Horizon HTML Access (or after installing the VMware Horizon Client), log in with your Truman account and select Windows 10 Virtual Desktop. This virtual desktop functions like a traditional Windows desktop environment with Microsoft Office installed.

Midterm Exams

The course includes four equally weighted examinations. Exam dates appear in the Detailed Tentative Schedule portion of this syllabus. During exams, you may use a calculator (cell phone and programmable calculators are NOT allowed) and any number of sheets of blank scratch paper that you must destroy at the end of the exam. At the end of the semester, you may replace your lowest test score of the four tests with the same grade earned on the Final Exam if the Final Exam grade is higher than the lowest test score. Only exams that were attempted can have the grade replaced. Exams will be available all day on the day reported in the schedule.

Final Exam

A final exam will be given during the scheduled final exam period. The date and time can be found in the Detailed Tentative Schedule portion of this syllabus. The material covered will be comprehensive. As with other exams, the final exam will be proctored via Proctorio.
