Course Overview and Expected Learning Outcomes

Your auditing course plays a unique role in your education. For some of you, this course is a stepping stone toward a CPA certification and a career in public accounting. For others, this course may seem like a detour on your chosen career path or even a hurdle in your way. While I don't want to discourage anyone who has their sights set on a career as a CPA, I do want to broaden everyone's perspective regarding the possible benefits of a good audit education. Fundamentally, auditing is about ethics, professional judgment, and skepticism. It asks the question, “Is everything as it seems?” The ability to see the business world with a critical eye is beneficial whether your future career is in auditing, tax, bookkeeping, sales, consulting, finance, or even data analytics. The need for ethics and professional skepticism is especially strong amid current and emerging threats to business information and information technology.

Believe it or not, the most important element of the business world to view with a critical eye is own our lives, so we will go beyond the subject of financial statement auditing to ask ourselves the question, “How will you measure your life?” Throughout the semester, we will take a break from the study of auditing to ponder this question.

Our study of auditing and how we will measure our lives will rely on five primary elements. (1) Reading the textbook will give you foundational understanding of auditing principles and practices. (2) Completing exercises out of class and cases in class will provide an opportunity to apply what you have read. (3) Watching curated videos will expand your awareness of information technology and control as a uniquely significant target of modern-day audits. (4) Reading a secondary guide will give you insight into how to measure your own life. (5) Discussing together what each student has learned will augment the learning process by leveraging each individual's effort to elevate one another.

As unpopular as it might be, learning begins with reading. Whether or not we realize it, most of our learning comes from the written word. Some exceptions exist, such as the IT videos that you will watch and the class discussions that we will have, but these exceptions can never supplant the importance of reading as much as we can. There are no shortcuts in learning. We get out of it what we put into it, so if you haven't already become converted to reading for learning, take a leap of faith and invest in your own self by reading in this course. It will be worth it; I promise.

Course Deliverables

Exams

This course includes four exams. You will complete exams in Blackboard in the Testing Center. Each exam is available from 8:00 am until 5:00 pm on the date reported in the tentative schedule. Exams will cover the same topics as the exercises. Where necessary, I will curve exams upward, but I will never curve any exams downward. We will have no class on exam days.

Exercises

For each ebook chapter, I will identify end-of-chapter exercises that serve to reinforce your understanding of what you read in the Auditing & Assurance Services ebook. You will complete these exercises in Connect. Exercises are graded based on accuracy and are due before class on the day listed in the tentative schedule. Exercises are due before class to promote independent learning and to enhance the value of class time. We may discuss any exercises in class and/or during my office hours.

Presentations

Each student will give two group presentations during the semester. The first presentation will be the solution to a case from the Auditing Cases ebook. Each group will select one case and will present on the day that case is listed in the tentative schedule. The second presentation will cover an information technology or security topic of your choosing. All groups will give these second presentations at the end of the semester as indicated in the tentative schedule. I will grade presentations according to this rubric.

Participation

Every student is responsible for learning independently through (1) reading the Auditing & Assurance Services ebook and (2) completing exercises. Class sessions also contribute to the collective learning process by providing opportunities to ask questions, discuss readings, and watch presentations. In order to encourage contribution to the collective learning process, part of your grade will be based on your in-class participation. Participation will be evaluated by attendance, in-class quizzes, in-class writing prompts, and/or in-class case discussions. Participation is especially important during student presentations to show respect and support for your classmates.

Extra Credit: SmartBook

For most chapters, I will assign a SmartBook adaptive learning assignment to assess your understanding of the chapter that you read. You can complete these exercises in Connect for up to 1 extra credit point per chapter. SmartBook assignments are graded on completion and are due before class on the day listed in the tentative schedule.

Extra Credit: Communication

I want us to work well together, so I will ask for communication from you during the semester for two reasons. First, I want to get to know you, so I will ask for you to introduce yourself to me in a letter that you will upload to Blackboard. I will not dictate a format for this letter, but I will invite you to use this experience to practice your professional writing skills, so please use correct grammar and spelling and a professional writing style. Second, I value continuous improvement. The purpose of this course is to help you to improve as professionals, but I also want to improve as a teacher, so I will give extra credit for the student evaluations that you provide at the end of the semester. You can complete these assignments for 1 extra credit point each.
