Course Overview and Expected Learning Outcomes

Accounting 4301 is the first auditing accounting course. If you plan for a career in accounting, auditing, or finance; corporate taxation or management consulting; or even data analytics, this is one of the most important courses you will take in your undergraduate program.

The more you invest in this course, the more prepared you will be for Intermediate II and your future career. You will get out of this course what you put into it. It is vital that you understand this material, not just try to memorize the answers to questions.

As unpopular as it might be, understanding the material begins with reading the textbook and completing exercises. The lectures will not substitute for a proper, self-motivated study of the textbook. However, I commit to doing my best to using class periods to maximize the value of a personal study of the textbook. If you will work with me, I will help you to have a successful experience.

Delivery Method

This course will have two in-person class periods each week. If any class periods must be held asynchronously, I will provide lecture notes, practice, and/or exercise or quiz review for each asynchronous class period to replace whatever would have been covered in class. I will notify you as early as possible if any upcoming class periods will be held asynchronously.

Communication

I will use Blackboard to post class-related documents and announcements. Announcements posted on Blackboard should automatically forward to your email address. If you wish to communicate with me, please send me an email to my Texas Tech address. For emails sent during the work week, my commitment to you is to respond to emails within 24 hours. For emails sent on Friday evening, Saturday, or Sunday, my commitment is to respond on Monday or the next business day.

Because this course has so many required materials, I have tried to provide them as inexpensively as possible. I have negotiated a reduced rate for the Auditing & Assurance Services book, and you can get the best price if you purchase the ebook using the Connect URL on Blackboard. The Auditing Cases ebook is available for rent on the Pearson+ library, and it is included in your subscription if you already pay for Pearson+ “Multi”. You can find digital or print copies of How Will You Measure Your Life? for under $10 at many book retailers. Finally, since I am the creator of the Udemy course, I am giving discounted access to that, as well.

Course Deliverables

Exams

This course includes four exams. You will complete exams in Blackboard in the Testing Center. Each exam is available from 8:00 am until 5:00 pm on the date reported in the tentative schedule. Exams will cover the same topics as the exercises. Where necessary, I will curve exams upward, but I will never curve any exams downward. We will have no class on exam days.

Exercises

For each ebook chapter, I will identify end-of-chapter exercises that serve to reinforce your understanding of what you read in the Auditing & Assurance Services ebook. You will complete these exercises in Connect. Exercises are graded based on accuracy and are due before class on the day listed in the tentative schedule. We may discuss any exercises in class and/or during my office hours.

Presentations

Each student will give two group presentations during the semester. The first presentation will be the solution to a case from the Auditing Cases ebook. Each group will select one case and will present on the day that case is listed in the tentative schedule. The second presentation will cover an IT security topic of your choosing. All groups will give these second presentations at the end of the semester as indicated in the tentative schedule. I will grade presentations according to this rubric.

Participation

Every student is responsible for learning independently through (1) reading the Auditing & Assurance Services ebook and (2) completing exercises. Class sessions also contribute to the collective learning process by providing opportunities to ask questions, discuss readings, and watch presentations. In order to encourage contribution to the collective learning process, part of your grade will be based on your in-class participation. Participation will be evaluated by attendance, in-class quizzes, in-class writing prompts, and/or in-class case discussions. Participation is especially important during student presentations to show respect and support for your classmates.

Extra Credit: SmartBook

For most chapters, I will assign a SmartBook adaptive learning assignment to assess your understanding of the chapter that you read. You can complete these exercises in Connect for up to 1 extra credit point per chapter. SmartBook assignments are graded on completion and are due before class on the day listed in the tentative schedule.

Extra Credit: Communication

I want us to work well together, so I will ask for communication from you during the semester for two reasons. First, I want to get to know you, so I will ask for you to introduce yourself to me in a letter that you will upload to Blackboard. I will not dictate a format for this letter, but I will invite you to use this experience to practice your professional writing skills, so please use correct grammar and spelling and a professional writing style. Second, I value continuous improvement. The purpose of this course is to help you to improve as professionals, but I also want to improve as a teacher, so I will give extra credit for the student evaluations that you provide at the end of the semester. You can complete these assignments for 1 extra credit point each.
