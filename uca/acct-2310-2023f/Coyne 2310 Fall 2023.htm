ABOUT THIS COURSE

Course Prerequisites

MATH 1390 (or a higher-level mathematics course) and either sophomore status or recommendation of advisor/instructor.

Course Description

The course covers the fundamental principles of accounting for business entities: analyzing transactions, recording accounting information, and preparing financial reports for use by investors and creditors. The course provides an overview of accounting for specific assets, liabilities, and stockholders’ equity.

 

Together, Principles of Accounting I and II (ACCT 2310 and ACCT 2311) serve as an introduction to accounting for those who will prepare accounting information and those who will use accounting information when making business decisions.

Course Objectives

·         Learn how to analyze a business transaction and record its impact on the company’s accounts using the accrual basis of accounting.

·         Learn about accounts, account classifications, and how account changes are recorded.

·         Understand how financial statements are prepared from the company’s accounting records.

·         Begin to interpret financial statements.

·         Learn specifics about accounting for various asset, liability, and equity accounts.

·         Learn about accountability and internal control.

Exams

This course will have six exams: five midterms and one comprehensive final. Each exam will count equally toward your overall Exams grade. Exams will be administered in class on the dates listed below in the Class Schedule. Exams will test the same concepts as the homework, meaning exams will not test any concepts that you have not already seen in prior homework assignments. Exams will contain a combination of conceptual and numerical questions. Based on the difficulty of an exam, I may curve the exam upward, but I will never curve an exam downward. Once I have determined whether an exam will be curved, I will announce the curve to the class.

 

Homework

Each reading assignment (as listed below in the Class Schedule) will have an accompanying homework assignment to complete on Connect. You must have Connect access in order to complete homework assignments. Homework assignments may contain a combination of multiple-choice questions, short answer questions, and workout problems. Each homework assignment will count equally toward your overall Homework grade.

 

Participation

Each class day (as listed below in the Class Schedule) will have an in-class deliverable. Your Participation grade will reflect both your attendance and your preparation for class from personal reading and study. I will announce and administer the in-class deliverable each class day. Each class day will count equally toward your overall Participation grade.

 

ACES

The accounting department has crafted a series of Accounting Career Essential Skills (ACES) to prepare students for careers in accounting and business. Each accounting course focuses on one skill, and the skill for this course is careers in accounting. You will complete this deliverable in multiple modules by watching videos, reading articles, and taking quizzes on Blackboard. The due date for this deliverable is reported below in the Class Schedule.

 

Extra Credit

In order to incentivize and award extra effort in learning this material, I will offer one extra credit SmartBook assignment on Connect each reading day (as listed below in the Class Schedule). SmartBook assignments are adaptive learning assessments that test your reading comprehension. You earn points based on completion. Each SmartBook assignment will count equally toward your overall Extra Credit grade. If I choose to offer any other extra credit opportunities, I will inform the entire class.

Academic Integrity Statement

The University of Central Arkansas affirms its commitment to academic integrity and expects all members of the university community to accept shared responsibility for maintaining academic integrity. Students in this course are subject to the provisions of the university’s Academic Integrity Policy, approved by the Board of Trustees as Board Policy No. 709 on February 10, 2010, and published in the Student Handbook. Penalties for academic misconduct in this course may include a failing grade on an assignment, a failing grade in the course, or any other course-related sanction the instructor determines to be appropriate. Continued enrollment in this course affirms a student’s acceptance of this university policy.

 

For purposes of this course, academic integrity is straightforward to define. Don’t do anything that you know is wrong. Don’t do anything that is dishonest. Don’t do anything that you would want to keep a secret. At all times, be honest, do the right thing, and invite others to do the same.
